Frequently Asked Questions

How do I subscribe?

We offer subscription to Canopy Care on a “pay as you go,” month-by-month basis at $60/month (plus applicable taxes). The minimum commitment is two months, due upon initiation, after which each following month is charged (roughly) every 30 days. You can cancel at any time, at which point your subscription service will conclude at the end of the current month’s cycle.

As part of the subscription, we offer in-home visits at $120/visit (plus applicable taxes). Your practitioner comes to your preferred location and brings their own equipment. Payment is due at time of service. In-home visits are strictly voluntary, and not a requirement for subscription.

We accept payment via credit and debit card only. We provide itemized receipts in the case that your insurance plan accepts Canopy’s service as a medical claim against your deductible, or for reimbursement from your Flexible Spending Account (FSA) and Health Savings Account (HSA).

What is a Pediatric Nurse Practitioner (PNP)?
A PNP is an Advanced Registered Nurse Practitioner who specializes in the diagnosis, treatment, and management of infants, children, adolescents, and young adults, ages 0-21 (this does including prescribing medications). He or she has to have either a Master’s of Science in Nursing or a Doctor of Nursing Practice from a reputable program to be allowed to sit for national board certification in pediatrics. Once this has been achieved, a PNP has to be licensed in the state he/she practices. Each state has different laws for how much PNPs can do, though they are fully capable from their training to act independently as primary care providers. All university programs, the National Association of Pediatric Nurse Practitioners, the American Association of Nurse Practitioners, as well as the Institute of Medicine agree that Nurse Practitioners should practice to the full extent of their education. Nurse Practitioners are known for being holistic and approaching their patients from all health aspects: physical well-being, mental health, nutrition, preventative practices (including integrative health), and the acute disease process. All of our PNPs are board-certified in Pediatrics.

Are we an insurance provider?
No, we are not an insurance provider. Our PNPs are trained to consult, diagnose, and treat to the best of their ability, and in accordance with our Best Practices. This does not constitute insurance in the event of an emergency or other acute illnesses. In such cases, our PNPs will be quick to advise patients to seek emergency services.

Do we accept insurance as payment?
No, we do not accept insurance. At Canopy, we don’t believe in holding families responsible if insurance does not cover our services. Instead of constantly worrying what your policy will or won’t cover, our subscription and flat fee model gives you certainty that you can receive the care your child needs at a reasonable cost to you.

When can I contact my dedicated PNP?
Anytime! Our PNPs are here to serve you when you need it most, or whenever you need it period. No appointments, no screening, just direct access to the care you need through messages, e-mail, and calls. If face-to-face interaction is required, we offer in-home visits at affordable, flat prices.

Do we have a brick-and-mortar clinic?
No, we do not operate out from a physical location. Our PNPs operate locally from their own homes or offices, and come to you in the event of a live meeting. In such cases, they are fully equipped with the standard medical equipment needed for primary care. All other communication occurs through messaging, e-mail, and telephone. In all cases you need not worry about traveling to and waiting at a physical clinic just to receive the care you need.
